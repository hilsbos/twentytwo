Push day · chest · shoulders · triceps

Pressing strength and a fuller chest and shoulders. Pause 1–2 reps short of failure on every set; lower for a 3-count.

3 × AMRAP

Hit the top of your range for 3 sets → move to the next variation. 3-second descent.

Pull day · back · biceps · posture

The one day that needs a setup. Anchor a sturdy door, table, or — best of all — a doorway bar (see note below). This is what builds a complete, upright physique.

01Horizontal row
3 × 8–12
Towel row on door→Row under sturdy table→Feet elevated→One-arm

Folded towel around a door handle, lean back, pull chest to hands. Keep the body in one line.

02Band pull-apart / face-pull
3 × 15–20
Loop band, arms out→Anchored face-pull

Rear delts — the posture muscles a desk destroys.

03Superman Y-T-W
3 × 10–12
Floor, arms in Y→T→W, squeeze

Lower back + mid-traps. Zero equipment.

Core day · trunk · 15–20 min

The day that ties the whole body together. Brace hard, resist the pull, breathe slow. Stop 1–2 reps short, lower for a 3-count, and own every position before you progress.

01Dead bug
3 × 8–12
Tabletop arms only→Opposite arm + leg→Heels to floor→Band-press dead bug

Anti-extension. Low back glued to the floor the whole time. Count per side.

02Band anti-rotation press
3 × 10–15
Tall-kneeling hold→Tall-kneeling press→Standing press→Split-stance press

Anchor the long band at chest height. Stand side-on; don't let it twist you. Count per side.

03Side plank
3 × 20–45s
Knees down→Full, feet stacked→Top arm reach→Hip dips

Anti-side-bend. Stack everything in one straight line, hips high. Hold per side.

+Hollow rock + V-tuck hold
3 × 20–40s
Rock 8–12 in a dish·V-tuck hold

Finisher. Rock in a tight dish, then balance the V-tuck on your sit-bones. Low back rounds, not arched.

How you get stronger (no weights needed)
  • 1
    Reps first, then harder
    When you hit the top of a rep range for all 3 sets, graduate to the next variation in the path. That's your "adding weight."
  • 2
    Slow the descent
    A 3-second lowering phase doubles the muscle-building stimulus with zero extra equipment. This is the bodyweight cheat code.
  • 3
    Stop 1–2 reps short
    Training close to failure is the trigger for growth. Close — not into ugly, form-breaking reps.
  • 4
    Write down your reps
    Beating last week's numbers is the whole game. The notes app is your progress tracker.
⊕  One upgrade closes the gap

Bodyweight + your loop band covers roughly 80% of a complete physique — legs and pushing are fully handled. The missing piece is pulling. A doorway pull-up bar or a set of long door-anchor resistance bands (~$20–25, both pack flat for travel) unlocks real back and bicep development. On pull days, that turns towel rows into dead hangs → negatives → your first pull-up.

FLOOR

No-excuse minimum: short on time or low energy? Do one round of the three main moves. Ten minutes, done. A consistent 10 beats a perfect 20 you skip.
